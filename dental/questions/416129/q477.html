Question 477 of 518 (Prosthodontics)

The fracture on the incisal edge is

Explanation #416129.395 (Scroll down to view)

The common porcelain laminate veneer failure types are static,cohesive, and adhesive fractures.

  • The static failure is fracture of a fragment of veneer while the remainder of the veneer remains intact on the tooth.
  • Thecohesivefracture is characterized by fracture occurring within the body (dentin) of porcelain ; also known as chipping fracture (a piece of porcelain), as a result of excessive functional or parafunctional loading.
  • In adhesive failure, the intact porcelain veneer debonds completely from the tooth (in case where a metal substrate is present as in the case of Metal-Ceramic Restorations- the metal will be exposed).
